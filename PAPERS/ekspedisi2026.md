## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The year 2026 marks the "Deployment Horizon" for the global economy. As Artificial Intelligence matures from experimental cloud-based models to practical, **agentic systems** capable of autonomous reasoning and problem-solving, the challenge for nations has fundamentally shifted [web:136]. The question is no longer *"Can we access AI technology?"* but rather *"Can we apply it safely, productively, and equitably at scale?"*

For Indonesia, this moment presents a defining strategic imperative: we must take the high-level potential of the "AI Cloud" and ground it firmly in the reality of our "Nusantara Soil"—transforming abstract digital capabilities into tangible economic tools that serve our 286.7 million citizens and amplify our 68.2% working-age population advantage [file:162].

**Ekspedisi AI Nusantara 2026** is the strategic vehicle designed to accomplish this mission. Initiated by Tsinghua Southeast Asia Center and United in Diversity, this is a **National Deployment Strategy** that directly operationalizes Indonesia's **National Strategy for AI (Stranas KA 2045)** [file:163]. We will "Ground the Cloud" by converting borderless AI capabilities into contextualized solutions that solve real problems for civil servants, farmers, SME operators, and startup founders across all 38 provinces [file:162].

To achieve this, we introduce a new, research-based pedagogical architecture: the **NUSA Framework (National Upskilling Sprint for AI)**, powered by the **IRIS Cycle (Immersion-Reflection-Iteration-Scale)**. This model moves beyond traditional academic training to deploy rapid, challenge-based sprints that produce **SFIA-mapped, ISO 17024-ready certified practitioners** prepared for the age of Agentic AI. The IRIS cycle is conceptually grounded in established learning science, integrating Kolb's experiential learning, Revans' action learning, and Lean Startup's build-measure-learn loop [web:30][web:27][web:55].

Our methodology creates a synchronized feedback loop spanning three temporal phases [file:162]:

1.  **THE CLOUD (June 2026):** Convening global technology leaders, national policymakers (Kemenkomdigi, KORIKA), and academic institutions in Bali to ratify the **"Nusantara AI Protocol"**—a harmonized standard for curriculum, ethics, and deployment aligned with Pancasila values [file:163]. This phase launches the **"Nusantara Digital Torch"**, creating a unified signal for national transformation.

2.  **THE GROUNDING (July–December 2026):** Executing a six-month relay across all 38 provinces, deploying the **NUSA Framework** via the T6 (TSEA-X) platform. We employ a **Triple-to-Hexa Helix** grounding strategy, ensuring the national protocol is transmitted directly to local governments, campuses, and SMEs before escalating to community and financial sectors for sustainable ecosystem building.

3.  **THE HORIZON (January 2027):** Culminating at the National AI Summit in Jakarta, where aggregated IRIS projects generate the **"Peta Potensi AI Nusantara"** (National AI Potential Map)—a sovereign, data-driven intelligence asset visualizing our nation's human capital distribution and investment opportunities [file:162].

The campaign operates under a lean, sustainable budget model with a baseline of IDR 6.6 billion (USD $400K), delivering [file:162]:

*   **100,000+ certified practitioners** mapped to the **SFIA global digital skills framework (Level 3/4)**, ready for both AI-augmented employment and SME entrepreneurship [web:145][web:127].
*   38 provincial relay events with standardized **NUSA/IRIS** delivery.
*   **Blockchain-verified credentials** aligned with international **ISO/IEC 17024** standards for certification of persons [web:157][web:150].
*   National media visibility through MURI record attempts [file:162].
*   Crowdsourced AI potential intelligence enabling sovereign strategic planning.

## 2. STRATEGIC CONTEXT & REGIONAL LANDSCAPE

### 2.1 The 2026 Deployment Horizon: Why Now?
Southeast Asia's AI ecosystem is entering a hyper-acceleration phase, projected to generate USD 835 billion in economic value by 2030, with compound annual growth exceeding 25% [file:162]. However, a critical bottleneck threatens this opportunity: across ASEAN, approximately 30% of AI-critical technical roles remain unfilled due to insufficient domestic talent pipelines [file:162]. In Indonesia specifically, the nation faces a need for up to 9 million digital professionals by 2030 to fully realize its digital economy potential [web:70].

The challenge is not technology access; it is **deployment capability**. Without immediate intervention, Indonesia risks a "K-shaped" AI adoption trajectory where elite institutions capture gains while rural regions and SMEs are left behind, widening the economic inequality chasm [file:162][web:140]. Ekspedisi AI Nusantara 2026 is the strategic intervention designed to invert this trajectory through mass, distributed upskilling.

### 2.2 Indonesia's Positioning Within ASEAN: The "Application Engine" Strategy
Indonesia operates within a competitive ASEAN AI ecosystem [file:162]. While Singapore positions itself as the **"R&D Hub"** and Vietnam as the **"Coding Factory,"** Indonesia's optimal strategy is to become the **"Application Engine."** This strategy leverages our three distinct competitive advantages:

1.  **Demographic Scale & Diversity:** With 286.7 million citizens and 42.4% under age 25, we have the raw human capital to develop millions of AI-literate practitioners [file:162].
2.  **Problem Density & Economic Complexity:** Our diverse economy—with 66 million SMEs, a vast agricultural sector, and a complex maritime landscape—is an ideal laboratory for impactful AI solutions [file:162].
3.  **Market Access & Regional Influence:** As ASEAN's largest economy, Indonesia is the natural test market for AI solutions at scale [file:162].

**Strategic Positioning:** Indonesia's advantage lies in **Scale and Application**. Ekspedisi AI Nusantara 2026 activates this by building 100,000 AI-literate practitioners, shifting Indonesia from a consumer of foreign AI products to a producer of indigenous AI solutions.

### 3.2 The "Long Game": Sovereignty, Resilience, and the AGI Horizon
Consistent with Stranas KA 2045, we proactively prepare for the **AGI Horizon**. The strategy document explicitly states, *"Since the beginning, it is necessary to consider the direction of AGI development for national interests"* [file:163]. We address this through three pillars of sovereignty:

1.  **Talent Sovereignty (Human-in-the-Loop Governance):**
    As AI systems become agentic, human oversight is critical [web:136]. The NUSA curriculum embeds **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** and **Human-in-Command (HIC)** principles, as defined in Stranas [file:163]. We train Fellows not just to build AI, but to govern it.

2.  **Data Sovereignty (Sovereign Intelligence):**
    The **Peta Potensi AI Nusantara** generates a domestically owned, real-time map of national AI capability. This reduces reliance on foreign consulting firms, ensuring the state possesses the intelligence needed to navigate the AGI transition independently.

3.  **Infrastructure Resilience (Survival AI):**
    The T6 platform employs a **"Survival AI"** design philosophy (Offline-First). This ensures that AI capability reaches every island, regardless of connectivity, protecting equity of access and resilience against external dependencies [file:162].

3.  **ITERATION (Week 3):** Teams build functional prototypes using **Build-Measure-Learn** loops on T6's AI toolchain.

## 6. ACCREDITATION & STANDARDS STRATEGY

To ensure international trust and high value, we align with global standards.

1.  **SFIA (Skills Framework for the Information Age):**
    We use SFIA, the global framework used in 200+ countries, as our competency spine [web:145][web:148]. All outcomes are mapped to **SFIA Level 3 (Apply)** and **Level 4 (Enable)**, making our graduates globally legible [web:125][web:127].

2.  **ISO/IEC 17024 Alignment:**
    The **Nusantara AI Fellow** certification scheme is **designed to be ISO/IEC 17024-ready**. We maintain organizational separation between training and certification, ensuring impartial, competence-based assessment and paving the way for formal accreditation [web:157][web:150].

3.  **Dual-Path Impact Strategy:**
    The NUSA curriculum prepares Fellows for two outcomes:
    *   **AI-Augmented Worker:** Ready for employment in modern firms.
    *   **AI-Enabled Entrepreneur:** Ready to launch new ventures or upgrade SMEs.
